Children across the U.S. are being unnecessarily put at risk by poorly trained dentists looking to increase their profits by sedating young patients for routine treatments.

Encouraging parents to choose the expensive pain free option for children anxious about dental treatment, some dentists have had as little as two days training in safe sedation which adds tens of thousands of dollars to the charge of the treatment.

In some cases dentists across the U.S. are administering oral sedatives on patients as young as 18-months old and one Virginia based foundation has compiled figures that suggest 31 children have died during or after dental treatment in the last 15 years alone.

On March 9, 2007, 8-year-old Raven Maria Blanco died in the dentist's chair after receiving a lethal dose of sedatives from her practitioner. Following her sudden death her parents set up a foundation in her memory

The Raven Maria Blanco Foundation was founded by Robin and Mario Blanco after the 2007 death of their eight-year-old daughter, Raven Blanco at the dentist, under sedation, during a routine dental procedure.

An investigation by the Virginia Board of Dentistry into her dentist Dr. Michael Hechtkopf, discovered that the little girl was given 'three times the average range' of sedatives normally given to a child of her weight and normal health.

The autopsy for Blanco revealed that she had a blood concentration of 24 mg/l of chloral hydrate, which the medical examiner considered to be lethal.

Following Blanco's death, Dr Hechtkopf had his license restricted for three months and was compelled to complete another seven hours of retraining in record keeping and risk management. 

On September 27, 2006, little Diamond Brownridge went to the dentist to have two cavities filled and her front, bottom teeth capped. The dentist administered nitrous oxide and an IV sedation and soon after, Diamond had no pulse

Speaking through a lawyer in the aftermath of the tragic death of eight-year-old Blanco, a lawyer for Dr. Hechtkopf said that his client 'regretted' what happened.

Creating the foundation to warn parents of the dangers of poorly prepared and trained dentists, the Blanco's campaign for a registry of deaths during dental treatment and to warn that what happened to their daughter could happen to others.

'Parents assume that a dentist should know what he's doing and that's not always the case,' said Blanco's parents on ABC News' 'World News with Diane Sawyer'.

During the course of their investigation, ABC News discovered that there was only a patchwork of state regulations on dentists, with states requiring only a weekend long course for dentists to be certified in administering oral sedatives.

On January 3rd 2011, 13-year-old Marissa Kingery collapsed and died in the offices of oral surgeon Dr. Henry Mazorow on West 21st Street in Lorain, Ohio. The coroner's report said that she had been given four different oral sedatives

Across the country, the Illinois Board of Dentistry found that a Dr. Hicham Riba administered an excessive dose of sedatives to five-year-old Diamond Brownridge who died after being put under, prior to having cavities and teeth capped.

'Who thinks that they're gonna take their daughter to the dentist and never bring her home,' said Diamond's mother Ommettress Travis, from Chicago.

Furthermore, the Illinois Board of Dentistry said that Dr. Riba, 'demonstrated a complete lack of understanding of conscious sedation', even though he had performed thousands of hours of dental work over nine years.

Speaking up for the profession, some dentists explain that sedation is routine for small children because of their fear of high speed drills and other oral surgery equipment.

Devastated by the death of his little girl, Mario Blanco (left and right) set up a foundation in Raven's name

However, they added that dentists should take extensive training to become proficient in the safe adminstration of sedatives and to deal with emergencies.

'This is something that is being presented to the practitioners, the dental community, as a very easy thing to do and nothing could be further from the truth,' said Dr. Robert Kaminski, a dental anesthesiologist from suburban Detroit.

Over the past five years, 18,000 dentists across America have taken a two-day course in oral sedation run out of local hotel conference rooms, with the promise that the training will add thousands to what they can charge their patients.

On February 8 of this year, Juan Quiej died from cardiac arrest during a standard dental procedure

Miciah Bonzani (left) died on May 5, 2011 in Pittsburgh, Pennsylvania, after an outpatient dental procedure, while Dylan Stewart died on the dentist chair on April 22, 2010 after being administered oral sedatives

'Pain-free dentistry can mean tens of thousands of dollars of extra income in your pocket annually and as much as half a million extra in your pocket at retirement,' wrote a Dr. Michael Silverman, one dentist who runs a firm that trains dentists in the use of oral sedation.

Countering that claim, a national spokesman for the American Academy of Pediatric Dentistry, Dr Indru Punwani of the University of Illinois said that a weekend course is 'inadequate' for any dentist to deal with emergencies that could arise from the use of oral sedatives.

'I don't believe that it can be done,' said Dr.Punwani of the courses which almost 20,000 dentists have taken over the past half-decade.

Dr. Silverman declined an interview but provided a written response that said, 'Everyone who has attended and completed any of our DOCS Education course knows that safety is by far our top concern and emphasis.'
