Sometimes, it can feel like anxiety is hiding behind every corner, waiting to pounce. Giving a presentation at work? Cue the trembling legs. Going to a social event where you don’t know anyone? Cue the sweaty palms. Stressing about everything on your never-ending to-do list? Cue the overnight tossing and turning. These thoughts and reactions to anxiety are totally normal, and millions of other people are undoubtedly experiencing them this very minute.

Now, there are certainly universal ways to cope, but we’ve all got our go-tos. Perhaps you phone a loved one, roll out a yoga mat, or pencil in an online therapy appointment. These are all great ways to manage anxiety, but what happens when you don’t have these solutions on hand?

To back up these TikTok picks, we spoke with two experts about what anxiety is, how it can manifest, and why certain products can assist in relieving nerves.

What is anxiety? 

“Anxiety is often defined as excessive worry, but there is a lot more to the emotion. Anxiety can present itself as fatigue, irritability, restlessness, sadness, and an inability to concentrate. Anxiety can range from very mild to severe,” says clinical and forensic psychologist Dr. Leslie Dobson, M.S., M.A., Psy.D.

How can you tell if it’s everyday stress or an actual anxiety disorder? “Anxiety is a normal and adaptive response that can help individuals cope with stressful or potentially dangerous situations,” says clinical psychologist Dr. Lisa Strohman, Ph.D. “However, when anxiety becomes excessive, persistent, and interferes with daily life, it can be classified as an anxiety disorder.”

What are the symptoms of anxiety?

As Dr. Strohman notes, symptoms vary from person to person, but common ones include: 

  • Extensive worry, whether it be about health, work, relationships, finances, or just about anything else
  • Physical symptoms such as increased heart rate, muscle tension, trembling, sweating, and gastrointestinal issues like nausea or diarrhea
  • Restlessness, which can present itself as feeling on edge, being unable to relax, and having trouble sitting still or focusing
  • Sleep disturbances like insomnia or disrupted sleep patterns 
  • Irrational fears or phobias—from a phobia of a specific object to social anxiety disorder (a.k.a. fear of social situations)
  • Avoidance behavior to dodge situations that trigger anxiety 
  • Panic attacks, which are intense episodes of fear or panic that can bring forward a racing heart, shortness of breath, and other negative emotions

How can I manage my anxiety?

Some people might be able to quell their anxious feelings by using the products we’re about to mention. But those with more intense feelings of anxiety or an actual anxiety disorder will likely need something stronger, like therapy or, in some cases, medication. None of the products below claim to completely eliminate anxiety; we always suggest consulting a professional.
