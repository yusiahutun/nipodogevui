The 13 Best Work Bags of 2024, Tested and Reviewed

Nothing is more frustrating than getting ready for work and realizing you can't fit everything in your bag. Or perhaps you can, but it'll feel like you are carrying a thousand bricks on your shoulders. When space is this precious, every little item counts, so it's between taking your water bottle or lunch box at this point.

Instead of taking whatever fits the most stuff regardless of looks, we found plenty of designs that don't force you to sacrifice fashion for function. We spoke with stylists and our very own fashion editors to bring you a list of tried and true work bags. Leather bags that promise durability, backpacks that free up your hands, and tote bags where it's compartment-galore on the inside are some of the best bags used by the staff here at InStyle and then some.

Best Overall

Caraa Studio Large Leather Tote

Caraa

What We Love: With the option to switch between a tote or backpack, you get the best of both worlds.

What We Don't Love: We wish it came in more colors than just black.

The ideal work bag should hold everything without feeling too heavy or becoming disorganized. The Caraa Studio Tote can do just that. "The Caraa tote is the best bag I've ever used for commuting. It fits everything I need — extra pair of shoes, lunch, water bottle — and if it gets too heavy, I convert it to a backpack," says Shannon Bauer, Senior Commerce Editor at InStyle. "It's also my go-to bag for travel when I need to bring my laptop along too." Bauer chose a medium size, which can hold a 15-inch laptop. She notes that bulky lunch containers take extra finagling because the bag is on the narrow side. Luckily, there's a larger size, which fits a 17-inch laptop. Both have seven different pockets and compartments to organize your belongings.

InStyle's former Senior Commerce Editor Chloe Anello recommends this bag, too. (Note: it was gifted to her a few years ago.) "I have a two-hour commute coming into the city, so I need something that can stand up to a lot of abuse on public transit. I've never used a more durable bag — I have yet to notice a major flaw after three years of constant use," she says. She owns the nylon fabric bag, as does Bauer because it's lightweight, waterproof, and easy to clean, but the bag comes in high-quality leather, too. You can even choose between gold or black hardware. We appreciate how wide the removable, leather backpack straps are. The wider the straps, the better they distribute weight, so they're less likely to dig into your shoulders, according to Los Angeles-based stylist Joanna Angeles.

Material: Nylon or leather | Dimensions: 12" H x 7.5" W x 17.5" L (medium) | Colors: 1

Best Leather

Madewell Transport Tote

Amazon

What We Love: Made from beautiful leather, this tote is surprisingly durable.

What We Don't Love: Due to its soft construction, the bag won't stand on its own.

For those who prefer leather, you can't go wrong with Madewell's Transport Tote. It's made from pebbled leather, which better fends off scuffs and scratches than smooth leather, according to New York City-based stylist Beverly Osemwenkhae. A number of our editors have used this tote throughout college and can confirm it withstands lots of wear and tear from lugging my books around. While it may not be the largest tote out there, it's still roomy enough to hold a 13-inch laptop, textbook, and other essentials. The compact size lends itself to more versatility — we've worn it off-duty, and it felt just like a regular purse.

The interior pocket keeps your keys and wallet secure while the slip pocket on the outside gives easy access to essential items, like a phone. The zipper at the top adds extra reassurance that nothing will fall out during a commute, and there are long shoulder straps for crossbody effect. Most importantly, however, we love the classic leather design — the black and mahogany shades also look luxe — and we've been pleasantly surprised with how functional the bag is, too.

Material: Leather | Dimensions: 12.75" H x 5" W x 11" L | Colors: 5

Best Lightweight

MZ Wallace Deluxe Tote

Nordstrom

What We Love: You can stuff this quilted bag to the brim without feeling weighed down.

What We Don't Love: There's no special compartment for your laptop.

While a sturdy, structured bag has its place, sometimes you need a design that is lightweight and packable — cue the MZ Wallace Metro Tote. This has been a bag we've been seeing all around the commuter trains in New York City. We asked around to see what all the fuss was about. Former InStyle writer Caitlyn Martyn describes the easy-to-clean nylon bag as Mary Poppins' never-ending bag. "I really like how flexible the bag is. I've been able to stuff not only work items [into the bag] but a change of clothes and a makeup bag, glasses, sunglasses, water bottle, etc. too, and it somehow always fits," Martyn says. Considering that it has five exterior pockets and six interior pockets, its capacity isn't that surprising. It doesn't have a dedicated space for your laptop, but a 13-inch laptop fits perfectly in the center. To prevent back pain, the nylon straps on this tote are padded on top of being wider than most. There's also a detachable crossbody strap.

Material: REC Oxford | Dimensions: 12.7" H x 9.4" W x 11.6" L | Colors: 18

Amazon

What We Love: Despite the low price, this tote is sturdy, protects your electronics, and looks stylish.

What We Don't Love: It doesn't have any pockets on the outside.

After seeing a blogger Martina Damico post about this bag, we assumed for how expensive it looked, it would go way over budget. Turns out, you can buy it on Amazon for less than $60. The faux leather material, minimalistic design, and neutral shades look elegant. On the inside, you'll find a padded sleeve in the center to keep a 15.6-inch laptop with additional pockets to hold your phone, keys, and other knick knacks usually rolling around in the bottom of your purse. And to prevent anything from spilling out, a magnetic clasp on the top keeps everything safe.

Reviewers appreciate that the tote stands up on its own thanks to the sturdy construction and flat bottom, which coincidentally makes it great for flights, too, because it can easily slide under the seat without falling over onto your feet. If you've ever had a bag with a dark or black interior, you'll know that makes things harder to find, especially when digging around mid-flight, so thankfully this design has light-colored fabric on the inside. And as notorious over-packers ourselves, we know from experience that when our bag is especially stuffed, it's uncomfortable to wear right under the armpit. With adjustable shoulder straps, you can simply increase the length for a more comfortable fit.

Our Testing Process

To find the best work bags, we tapped style experts and InStyle editors to learn what they're toting to the office in 2024. We surveyed each person on the brand and style of their favorite bags and had them elaborate on their durability, organizational capabilities, and ease of use. Some bags were submitted more than once, guaranteeing their spots on this list. Others offered unique features, like an extra strap or a removable pouch, which earned it its spot on this list as well.

What to Keep in Mind

Straps

Straps are the most important part of a bag, according to Angeles. As a stylist, she always totes around a lot of items, so she knows the importance of comfortable and strong straps. "Check to make sure the stitching of the straps look secure and that [they] don't pull a lot at the seams holding the weight of the bag," Angeles advises, adding, "Avoid thin handles and shoulder straps." And if you still feel too much pressure on your shoulders, Angeles recommends a backpack that distributes weight more evenly. Several options on this list, including the Coach Rae Tote and the Polène Cyme Tote offer at least two straps or ways to carry the bag, be it via a handle or strap. But of course, if you want to skip the straps altogether, you can always opt for the Dagne Dover Dakota Neoprene Backpack.

Material

A bag's fabric can completely change its form and function. Angeles says nylon, found in the MZ Wallace Metro Deluxe Tote, is one of the most durable and lightweight materials, plus it's fairly easy to clean. It tends to look more casual than leather, so if you prefer a more elevated bag, Osemwenkhae recommends splurging on a leather bag, like Markle's favorite Everlane Tote. She suggests opting for pebbled leather, too, since it's more durable and doesn't show scuffs or scratches easily.

Pockets

If you take a laptop or tablet to work, look for a bag with a laptop compartment to keep it secured. (We recommend the Freja Paloma Tote in this case.) Be sure to measure and double-check that your device will actually fit inside, especially while your bag is already filled with other stuff. Pockets on the outside, in addition to the inside, will be helpful for small items like keys, hair ties, and a phone. For an added level of security, seek out a zip-top or button closure to ensure nothing can get in or out when in a crowded space.

Your Questions, Answered

How do I extend the life of my bag?

To make your bag last, first things first: Don't hang your bags, as it ultimately wears down the straps. Angeles recommends keeping them on a shelf, ideally in a dust bag with some sort of stuffing inside to maintain the shape, in a cool, dry, and dark place to avoid fading.

How do I clean a bag?

Before cleaning your bag, check the label for any specific instructions. Angeles says you can usually clean a nylon or faux leather bag with a warm, damp towel and mild detergent or soap. Follow up with another damp towel to wipe off the soap and be sure you dry the bag completely with a dry towel. Unfortunately, cleaning real leather can be tricky, so Angeles recommends taking it to the professionals.
