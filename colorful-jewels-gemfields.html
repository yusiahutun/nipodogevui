The Ultimate Guide to Styling Colorful Jewels

Dopamine dressing is a trend that's here to stay, and colored gemstones are a sophisticated way to meet the mark. Emerald green and ruby red pieces were all over the runways this year, with top designers like Sandy Leong, Fehmida Lakhany, and Fabergé featuring the gemstones in their collections.

"Colors are inherently playful and colored gemstones can be a source of fun as well," says gemmologist, Elena Basaglia of Gemfields, one of the world's top suppliers of responsibly sourced gemstones. "Whether you combine them or wear them with diamonds, they complement each other. Anything goes!"

Read on for Harper's Bazaar UK fashion editor Tilly Wheating's top tips for successfully styling yourself with rubies and emeralds.

Should I choose gemstones that match my coloring?

"Look for gems that you love and will enjoy wearing," says Wheating. "If you have green eyes, emeralds will enhance them—but don't get stuck on what works with your hair or eye color; a bright gem will elevate any look."

People often gravitate to gemstone colors that make them feel good: Red fuels passion and desire, while green is associated with spring and renewal.

Can I wear rubies and emeralds with a casual look?

"I love pairing a white shirt and jeans with a ruby necklace or emerald drop earrings," Wheating says. "It's unexpectedly playful and elevates a casual look to something totally luxurious."

Great options that can be worn with any ensemble include a gold choker necklace with a row of rubies or a diamond collar with an emerald drop. Emerald or ruby earrings also add glamor to the everyday.

Can I combine different colored stones?

"Don't be shy about mixing gemstone hues," says Wheating. "New styles show rubies paired with turquoise and emeralds set alongside coral. That contrast of color highlights the richness of the gems."

Getty Images

Earrings by Bina Goenka

Mixing and matching is great, but a monotone look is also a powerful statement. If you are in head-to-toe black or camel clothing, consider choosing just emeralds or rubies for a sophisticated look.

How can I make colored gemstones edgy?

"Mismatched earrings add edginess," says Wheating. "Wear different shapes and sizes, such as a long ruby earring in one ear and a ruby stud in the other."

Also try layering colorful bracelets or necklaces. Start with one large anchor piece, such as a bold cuff or pendant necklace, and add more necklaces or bracelets around it.

What’s the easiest way to dress up with colored gemstones?

"The little black dress is the perfect backdrop for a sparkling collar or a long necklace with ruby or emerald beads," says Wheating. That being said, she adds, "Don’t be afraid to mix clashing colors: wear a green dress with rubies or a red dress with emeralds."

Getty Images

Necklace by Fabergé; Earrings by Foliage; Evil Eye bracelet by House of Maraki; Artesia Ring by GFG

If you are wearing a backless gown, a long, colorful necklace draped down a bare back is stunning. Don't forget statement earrings or ear crawlers set with bright gemstones.

What makes a good investment piece?

"I try to find the most timeless piece, one that I know I will wear for many occasions and can style with various looks," Wheating shares.

Look for gems you can live in; a ring is a wise investment because it becomes your signature jewel that can be worn every day.
