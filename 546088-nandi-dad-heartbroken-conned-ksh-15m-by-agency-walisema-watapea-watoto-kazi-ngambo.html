Nandi Dad Heartbroken after Being Conned KSh 1.5m by Agency: &quot;Walisema Watapea Watoto Kazi Ng'ambo&quot;

  • Joshua Lagat had sold his property to raise money to pay a travelling agency that promised his sons jobs in Canada
  • He paid at least KSh 1.5 million to Novice Global Service Consultants in the hope his kids will get employed abroad
  • However, he received a document saying that, unfortunately, his children did not qualify for work overseas

A loving and caring father has been left shattered in an attempt to help his children.

Job opportunities in Canada promise

Joshua Lagat claimed a travelling company, Novice Global Service Consultants, promised job opportunities in Canada.

However, the agency failed to deliver on the promise, leaving the father among others who had trusted with their money licking their wounds.

"They opened this office last year. They said their work was to help children go to work abroad. We went to the office and talked to them, and they explained what was required from our end. They said we could pay them to cater for everything if we had money. They said if we paid at least KSh 350,000, it would cater for everything," the father from Nandi County told Hubz Media.

The money would then be used for the application, visa, and the kids' security abroad until they get a work permit.

Nandi father sells property

Lagat said the payment was made last year, 2023, before going home to wait for the company's feedback.

"I was happy when I received some documents recently, hoping it was good news. However, we were told we were unlucky and did not qualify," he said.

Lagat said he spent KSh 1.5 million, which he raised by selling property in the hope of helping his three sons.

However, the shattering news left him in disappointment and despair, like many others who had trusted the company.

George Maina, a father of two from Nakuru told TUKO.co.ke he sympathised with the father trying to help his sons.

"Parents will always want the best for their kids and would go the extra mile to see their kids have better lives by making the most of every opportunity. however, I would urge parents to be cautious. This man sold his property which may have been part of his will to his sons but now it is no more. As much as we parents may be desperate at time, let us be careful," he said.
