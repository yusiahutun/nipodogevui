Jonathan Fisher's biography

The actor was born in 1986 in Coral Gables, Florida, making him around 36 years old (as of 2022). Despite being an actor, he has not revealed much information about himself, including his parents, siblings, and his exact date of birth.

Jonathan has made a few details about himself available since appearing on the Jeopardy TV show. One of them is that he attended St. Stephen's Episcopal Day School in Coral Gables. Later, he attended Ransom Everglades, one of Florida's top college prep schools. While at Ransom Everglades, Fisher had an impressive theatre career at the school, appearing in 10 school plays.

Aside from drama and theatre, Fisher was greatly interested in Jeopardy while growing up. In an interview following his appearance on the show, he admitted being a fan of the likes of Ken Jennings during his impressive 2004 winning streak. It is thus not surprising that he would seek to compete in the game show years later.

Career beginnings in theatre projects

After graduating from Northwestern University, Fisher lived in Providence, Rhode Island. During this time, he participated in several plays in the local Providence theatre scene. However, after three years in the city, he moved to California, where his partner had landed an acting gig.

Fisher has been living in California, where he is heavily involved in the local theatre scene. He has participated in several plays performed in theatres in California and surrounding states. Films

Aside from theatre, Fisher has also enjoyed a brief career as a film actor. He had a brief role in the 2011 horror mystery film, Absentia, where he portrayed a police officer. The other highlight of Jonathan Fisher's career has been his appearance in Jeopardy. He had been applying to participate in the show since 2018.

He finally got his chance in 2020 when he was invited for an in-person audition. After a few hiccups due to the Covid-19 Pandemic, Fisher finally got the opportunity to appear on the show in 2021. Fisher's debut in Jeopardy was on October 11, 2021.

On his first game on the show, he defeated champion Matt Amodio, who had been on a 38-game winning streak. The win earned him the nickname "longest streaking giant killer." His 11 wins also helped him achieve the record of the 13th longest winning streak on the show.
