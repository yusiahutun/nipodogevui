DJ Evolve Virtually Testifies in Babu Owino's Case from His Bed: &quot;I Knew Him in 2014&quot;

  • Felix Orinda Odhiambo alias DJ Evolve was cross-examined by Embakasi East MP Babu Owino's lawyer Duncan Okatch
  • In his testimony, Evolve said that he was friends with Owino since 2014 and they had neither quarreled nor had a fight before
  • He conformed that the legislator had been taking care of him since the shooting incident that left him bedridden, besides paying his utility bills
  • During his testimony, Owino told the court that he had paid all hospital bills for Evolve, bought him a house and was planning to take him to India for further treatment

It was an emotional moment on Thursday, June 2022, when Felix Orinda alias DJ Evolve, virtually joined court proceedings of the case involving Embakasi East MP Babu Owino.

Babu Owino, DJ Evolve's friendship

Owino, officially known as Paul Ongili was charged with misuse of a firearm in an incident that happened on the dawn of January 17, 2020.

In the incident DJ Evolve sustained serious bullet wounds that have left him bedridden to date.

While responding to Owino's lawyer Duncan Okatch, Evolve gave his account of events of the fateful night, saying that he was talking with the MP who was his friend since 2014, and the next moment he found himself in an ambulance.

DJ Evolve's shooting incident

"On the morning of January 17, I was at Club B and I was just having a conversation. Then, the next moment I found myself in an ambulance and on my way to hospital. I didn't know exactly what had happened," said Evolve.

The DJ told the court that they had never had squabbles or any kinds of fights with the MP since their friendship brewed in 2014 began.

Asked about submissions made by Owino, that he had been taking care of his needs and paying his utility bills, Evolve said that the MP was taking care of him as he had stated.

During his testimony, after the court found that he had a case to answer, Owino told the court that he had spent at least KSh 58.6 million on Evolve.

He also disclosed that the money included a KSh 17 million apartment he had bought for Evolve in Kilimani.

The MP also told the court that he was planning to take Evolve to India for further treatment, buy him a car and start him a DJ academy to help him become financially independent and pursue his passion.
