Sexologist Dr Nikki Goldstein on successful friends with benefits arrangement

I was in his bed, cuddled up to his chest, still slightly sweating from the adults-only activities we had just participated in, when the inevitable question came: 'So, what are you looking for exactly?'

He phrased it casually, but it hung in the air like a bad smell. This was, after only three dates, 'the talk'. He wanted to know if I wanted a relationship, or just something more casual, like a 'friends with benefits' type situation.  

For those out of loop with slang, friends with benefits is the term loosely given to a casual, no-strings-attached arrangement where two consenting adults have sex without any intention of forming a romantic or exclusive partnership.

Having some fun: Friends with benefits is the term given to two people who have casual sex with no intention of starting a romantic relationship

It's well referenced in pop culture, with a film called Friends With Benefits starring Mila Kunis and Justin Timberlake even being released in 2011.

And here was a guy I had just slept with asking me, with no preamble, if that's what I wanted. 

I guess I had met him on Tinder. 

But even before the advent of dating apps, friends with benefits was a fairly common arrangement struck between two individuals.

HOW CAN YOU HAVE A GOOD FRIENDS WITH BENEFITS RELATIONSHIP? 

  • Communicate with the person you're sleeping with and be honest
  • Be honest with yourself about your own feelings
  • Know that you might get hurt
  • Remember to have a friendship first and a sexual relationship second
  • Set boundaries and rules if needed
  • Realise that if you're prone to jealousy it may not work for you
  • It's supposed to be easy and fun, so if it's not anymore consider changing the arrangement
  • It can also make you emotionally unavailable for a real relationship, since you're focusing on someone else

Tallulah, a 28-year-old woman from Melbourne, had a friends with benefits relationship with an older man for some months after a break up.

'I was on holiday where he was living,' she told Daily Mail. 'I knew he thought I was cute and I was newly single so I just texted him and went to his place for a drink.'  

Part of the reason it worked so well, she said, was that they lived in different states so there was no chance of getting too attached. 

'I think it worked so well because nobody caught feelings. We liked each other and the attraction was there, but it never felt like there was a risk of getting hurt,' she explained. 

This, according to Sydney-based sexologist Dr Nikki Goldstein, is one of the biggest risks of entering into a friends with benefits arrangement. 

'Lust can get confused with love,' she said. 'Women especially can get hurt, because they can get attached. Women form an attachment because there is a positive association, so it's risky.'

Some people who have had successful arrangements firmly believe in setting boundaries, so as to ensure people don't get hurt. 

'Lust can get confused with love': Sexologist Dr Nikki Goldstein (above) says that friends with benefits can work, but warns that people can get hurt from the arrangement

Long distance lust: Tallulah said that because she lived in a different state to her friend with benefits, it made it easier to ensure no one developed romantic feelings or an attachment

Jason, a 20-year-old from Florida, is currently in a friends with benefits situation. He said that when he started to sleep with a friend of his, they set some loose rules. 

'She wanted to keep things private, so we never talked about our intimacy around others,' he said. 'We told one another about the other people we were going on dates with to make sure they never caused problems with our friendship.'

He said that his recommendation for anyone wanting a friends with benefits relationship was to be honest first and foremost.

'I think the most important thing is honest communication,' he explained. 'Knowing [the girl] for years beforehand made that kind of honesty easy, but I have been that open with many friends I find attractive with varying degrees of success.'

The first rule of friends with benefits is: Jason (above), is currently in a friends with benefits relationship where there are loose rules and boundaries to try and make the arrangement simpler

Rom-com gold: The film Friends With Benefits (above) centered around two friends who started having casual sex but fell in love

Whilst Dr Goldstein agrees that honesty is important, she stresses that ensuring you're honest with yourself is key. 

'You need to check in with yourself,' she said. 'You can set your boundaries but if your feelings start changing those boundaries don't mean a lot.' 

'When you've got a clear understanding of what the boundaries are, friends with benefits is great... it took a few goes at it to learn that.'

'If things change you need to flag it with the other person. otherwise it may come out in other ways. He might think it’s okay to tell you about this date he went on but that might upset you. So you need to be honest.'

The arrangements aren't always easy and fun however. Lizzie, a 27-year-old from Geelong, has had good and bad experiences with friends with benefits relationships.

She said that sometimes guys called their relationship friends with benefits because they didn't want to be exclusive, and she went along with it, when other times she simply slept with friends who she didn't have romantic feelings towards. 

'When you've got a mutual respect and clear understanding of what the boundaries are, friends with benefits is great,' she explained. 'Unfortunately it took a few goes at it to learn that.'

'I made the mistake of catching feelings more than once and not disclosing that, thinking it'd scare people off. But on one occasion that evolved into an actual long term relationship.'

Communication is key: Jason says that the most important thing in his opinion is being honest with the person you're sleeping with, but Dr Goldstein says you also need to be honest with yourself

'When you've got a mutual respect and clear understanding...friends with benefits is great': Lizzie (above) has had both good and bad experiences entering into a friends with benefits relationship

Lizzie says that she thinks the key to a friends with benefits arrangement is remembering being friends first, and having sex second. 

Whilst in Lizzie's case she said one friend with benefits ended up being a long term partner, Dr Goldstein says to be careful as this isn't often the case.

'I don’t think friends with benefits have a good chance of becoming a long term partnership because you don’t have the foundations needed for a good relationship,' she explained. 

'You need mutual respect, communication, courtship. If you build a relationship based solely on sexual chemistry then it may not last when put under pressure.' 

They're important points to seriously consider before entering into a friends with benefits arrangement, and something many people don't fully understand. 

As for me? My Tinder date is now one of my best friends, after a very successful period of enjoying each other's company. What started out as benefits ended as friends.
