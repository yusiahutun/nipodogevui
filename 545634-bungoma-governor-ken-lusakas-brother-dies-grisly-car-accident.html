Bungoma Governor Ken Lusaka's Brother Dies in Grisly Car Accident

  • Bungoma governor Kenneth Lusaka sympathised with his mother, siblings, and extended family members following the death of Noah Sikhila
  • Lusaka described Sikhila as a distinguished gentleman whose demeanour impressed everyone around him
  • The Roads and Transport Cabinet Secretary, Kipchumba Murkomen, had highlighted the urgent need to reduce road carnage

Nancy Odindo, a journalist at TUKO.co.ke, brings more than three years of experience covering politics, news, and feature stories across digital and print media in Kenya.

Tragedy has struck the family of Bungoma governor Kenneth Lusaka with the loss of their loved one in a devastating car accident.

Governor Lusaka revealed the heartbreaking news of his brother, Noah Sikhila Lusaka, passing on Sunday, April 21.

In his statement, the county leader conveyed deep sorrow and extended his sincere condolences to their mother, siblings, and friends, acknowledging the profound grief they are enduring.

"I am deeply saddened to announce the untimely demise of my beloved brother, Noah Sikhila Lusaka, in a car accident. My utter sympathies go to my mother, siblings, friends, and the extended family during this awfully difficult time," he said.

How did Kenneth Lusaka eulogise his brother?

He mourned Sikhila as a "distinguished gentleman" whose demeanour impressed everyone around him.

"As we grieve this profound loss, I find solace in cherishing the outstanding memories we shared. He leaves an indelible mark that will forever remain engraved in our hearts," he added.

Lusaka, however, did not disclose the scene of the accident.

The loss of Sikhila adds to the grim toll of road accidents, which have claimed over 1,200 lives since January 2024.

The Roads and Transport Cabinet Secretary, Kipchumba Murkomen, previously emphasised the urgent need to reduce road carnage.

Kenya mourns Francis Ogolla, nine other KDF soldiers

Meanwhile, the country is still mourning the demise of Chief of Defence General Francis Ogolla and nine other KDF soldiers who perished in a tragic chopper crash.

They 10 were on a mission to inspect the renovation of schools in the North Rift region ahead of schools reopening for the second term.

General Ogolla who died while still serving as the CDF, will be buried in his rural home in Ng'iya, Alego-Usonga Constituency in Siaya county.
