Serial toilet clogger' gets 150 days behind bars and three years probation for criminal damage

Patrick D. Beeman, 26, became known as a 'serial toilet clogger'. He was arrested after a year-long investigation and pleaded no contest to five misdemeanor charges

A Wisconsin man who became known as 'the serial toilet clogger' has been sentenced to 150-days in jail and three years probation after he was found guilty of criminal damage by deliberately blocking the ladies toilets at his workplace, ensuring they would overflow.

Patrick D. Beeman, 26, of Sheboygan, was convicted of five counts of criminal damage to property after he repeatedly shoved plastic bottles down the toilet bowl in the women's restroom.

The blockage would then see the contents spill out onto the floor with the companies having to fork out $200 for repairs each time. 

Beeman originally faced 12 counts of criminal damage to property, but then seven of the charges were dismissed in early June, according to the Sheboygan Press. 

'I need to make things right and pray for forgiveness every day,' Beeman said in court Monday. 

He told police that 'he could not explain this behavior, but simply that he would get very strong urges to do this,' court documents state, according to Fox 6.

Beeman told police he had urges to do 'odd' things, such as looking for bottles in the garbage to plug toilets. He said he stopped when he heard police were investigating.

Police began their investigations into Beeman after a toilet in a women's bathroom at Deland Community Center was clogged by a plastic bottle in March 2018.

A further ten similar instances at the center were also reviewed.

Sheboygan police even posted several times about the incidents on Facebook, hoping to catch the clogger. 

When he was finally arrested, Beeman was 'reluctant to admit to being responsible for the toilet clogging, but stated that he remembered doing approximately two of them.'

Officers informed him that there had been at least 12 reported cases whereupon he 'acknowledged that he was responsible for likely that many.' 

Joe Kerlin, the city’s parks and forestry superintendent complained that the bottles had been shoved down the pipes so firmly that repairmen had to take the toilets apart to remove them. 

State prosecutors recommended a 30-day sentence for Beeman but Judge Kent Hoffmann wanted there for be a more severe punishment.

Beeman will now serve 150 days in jail, pay $5,500 in fines, complete 100 hours of community service and will be on probation for three years, during which time he will not be allowed to possess or consume alcohol.

The manager of a temp agency where Beeman worked also came forward and told how he also damaged the toilets at the company where he was placed by the agency. 

After Beeman was moved to another company, more instances of clogged toilets began to occur, court documents state.
