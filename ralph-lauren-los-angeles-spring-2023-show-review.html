Ralph Lauren's California Dreams

It’s a sentiment plenty of New Yorkers shared during Peak Pandemic times: “Should I head out West?” For many, the city felt like it was closing in, and the siren songs of blue skies, green pastures, and, most importantly, wide open spaces seemed louder and louder as the months went on. Even quintessential New Yorker Ralph Lauren wasn’t immune. “I have always been inspired by the natural beauty, the heritage, and glamour of the West Coast of America,” the designer writes in his show notes for his Spring 2023 collection. “For the first time ever, I bring my dream of living here, sharing my worlds in an experience that celebrates a way of life I have always believed in—a mix of grit and glamour, energy and inspiration,”

And so off we went to The Huntington Library, Art Museum, and Botanical Gardens in San Marino, California, to take in Lauren’s ode to all things California.

Courtesy of Ralph Lauren

Diane Keaton at the Ralph Lauren spring 2023 show.

Joined by A-list celebs like Jennifer Lopez, Ben Affleck, Diane Keaton, Sylvestor Stallone, Laura Dern, Mila Kunis, and John Legend, plus TikTokers like Noah Beck, Wisdom Kaye, and Emily Mariko, editors sat on benches and wicker chairs, observing the many facets of Lauren’s empire—from Double RL and Polo Ralph Lauren to Ralph Lauren Collection and Purple Label. More than 100 models walked the runway, including icons like Shalom Harlow and Irinia Shayk, but also one tiny kid who stole the show (and the audience’s hearts), waving as he passed by.

Courtesy of Ralph Lauren

Shalom Harlow walking the Ralph Lauren spring 2023 show.

Courtesy of Ralph Lauren

Irina Shayk walking the Ralph Lauren spring 2023 show.

Adesuwa Aighewi opened the show wearing a tan suit and wide brim cowboy hat, making way for the Double RL portion of the event, which was meant to highlight the rugged workwear of the Old West. Beautifully beat-up cowboy boots and big buckle belts completed many of the looks. But before the audience got stuck in the Wild Wild West, the lineup quickly moved on to California’s more elegant and glamorous sides. Prairie dresses made way for turtlenecks and teeny white shorts (evoking memories of those classic Lauren photographs), slouchy all-white suits, racerback gowns, and sharp blazers paired with smoking shoes for men.

Courtesy of Ralph Lauren

A model wearing a sequin racerback gown at the Ralph Lauren spring 2023 show.

From there we shifted to Polo Ralph Lauren, arguably the most impactful moments of the entire presentation. Families took to the runway; cute kids made the audience gasp, and the styling showcased the wide breadth of what the world of Ralph has to offer. It was here we saw the So-Cal surfer vibes and Nor-Cal outdoorsy vibes the state is so well-known for. Tie-dye sweatshirts and bucket hats, teamed with puffers vests, jumpsuits, and hiking boots, drove things home.

Courtesy of Ralph Lauren

A family of models walking the Polo Ralph Lauren segment of the Ralph Lauren spring 2023 show.

As the show ended, instead of a single file runway finale walk, models lined the stairs of space and Lauren took a bow to a standing ovation. At 83, it can seem like the designer has done it all—but there’s still no telling what he’ll do next.
