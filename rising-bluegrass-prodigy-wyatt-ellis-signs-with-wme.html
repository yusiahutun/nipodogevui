Rising Bluegrass Prodigy Wyatt Ellis Signs With WME

Front Row (L-R): Bobby Cudd (WME), Jay Williams (WME), Wyatt Ellis, Neil Mason (Red Light Management), Tom Lord (Red Light Management) Back Row (L-R): Moira McCravey (WME), Carter Green (WME), Alison Auerbach (Alison Auerbach Public Relations), Erin Morris Huttlinger (Morris Public Relations)

NASHVILLE, TN (CelebrityAccess) — Following the successful release of his debut album, Happy Valley, the up-and-coming bluegrass artist Wyatt Ellis has signed on with WME for global representation.

At WME, Ellis’ team includes Bobby Cudd, Jay Williams, Moira McCravey and Carter Green. He is also repped by Red Light Management’s Neil Mason and Tom Lord. Morris Public Relations and Alison Auerbach P.R. are collaborating on artist development and public relations, while Andrea Roberts with the Andrea Roberts Agency is handling radio promotion for Ellis’ music.

“Wyatt is an extraordinary talent. He embraces tradition with his artistry while captivating a new generation of bluegrass fans. His future is very bright, and we’re excited to have him at WME and be a part of his journey,” his team at WME said in a joint statement.

“Wyatt is a breath of fresh air, and we are thrilled to be working with him,” added Red Light’s Neil Mason. “He is traditional and progressive, young, but seasoned well beyond his years. Wyatt has quickly made a ton of noise in the bluegrass and country scene, jamming with the likes of Billy Strings, Dierks Bentley, Del McCoury and many more, as well as performing at Eric Clapton’s Crossroads Guitar Fest, MerleFest, and the Grand Ole Opry, all in recent months.”

Along with his debut album, which dropped on February 2nd via his own label, Knee High Records, Ellis has been announced for multiple festival plays this year, including Big Ears Festival, MerleFest 2024, Floyd Fest, Ogden Music Festival, and Southern Skies Music Festival, with others to be announced soon.
