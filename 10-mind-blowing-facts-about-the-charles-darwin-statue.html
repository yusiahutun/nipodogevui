The Charles Darwin Statue is a Symbol of Scientific Achievement

The Charles Darwin Statue, located in London’s Natural History Museum, stands as a testament to the revolutionary ideas and scientific breakthroughs made by the renowned biologist, Charles Darwin.

The Statue Represents Darwin’s Theory of Evolution

Designed by Sir Joseph Boehm, the statue depicts Charles Darwin with a book in hand, symbolizing his groundbreaking work on the theory of evolution.

The Statue Took Over Three Years to Complete

It took sculptor Sir Joseph Boehm over three years to create the Charles Darwin Statue, which was unveiled in 1885.
