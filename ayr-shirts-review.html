AYR's Button-Down Shirt Is Timeless

There are as many places to shop for wardrobe essentials as there are women to wear them. But editors, models, and even Oprah Winfrey agree that AYR is the first and only place to find the perfect button-down shirt.

AYR has carried "The Deep End," a slightly oversized button-down in solid cotton poplins and vibrant, cabana-style stripes, since it opened nine years ago. Yet the $125 shirt is more popular than ever in 2021. On Instagram, a parade of influencers and fashion insiders like Eva Chen can be found in AYR shirts. Sometimes it's half-tucked into jeans, other times it's paired with coordinating striped shorts. Winfrey, after wearing and re-wearing her blue and white "Deep End" shirt throughout 2020, bestowed it with one of shopping's highest honors this year: a spot on her annual favorite things list.

Courtesy of AYR

To AYR's designers and the brand's followers, this button-down shirt has always been one ingredient in an easy outfit: polished, yet not overcomplicated or over-styled. In colorful stripes with names like "macaroni" (a bright yellow) and "grape soda" (a pale lavender), the "Deep End" was also a one-and-done way to inject optimism and energy into a classic. "It’s for people who don’t chase every trend, but know themselves and know what they like," AYR co-founder and CEO Maggie Winter tells BAZAAR.com on call with fellow co-founder Max Bonbrest.

Then times changed—and the ease of this shirt's loose fit and rolled sleeves became even more relevant. "I think Zoom culture’s impact has benefited our business in a huge way, and we saw this item really take off in the last year and a half," Winter says. "We haven’t been able to keep it in stock."

Many brands say they're all about effortless dressing. AYR's "Deep End" shirt actually provides it, according to women who own it and wear it often.

"This shirt is the ultimate versatile layer," says Katie Muirhead, an NYC-based model and writer. "I wear it done up with the sleeves rolled, as an over-shirt, or under a knit jumper."

BAZAAR.com digital director Nikki Ogunnaike found her shirt like so many other millennials—through Instagram. And it wasn't all she ordered. "I got the boxer short and 'Deep End' top set, which I love to wear together during the summer on a casual Friday," she says, "but when I want to dress it up I wear it with trousers."

I also dove into the "Deep End" after seeing it over and over social media. The white Oxford version was so comfortable (and easy to clean), I ordered the blue and white stripe version two weeks later.

"I do think it’s in the small details, like the proportion and the scale of the stripe that makes it recognizable," Winter concludes about the shirt's ever-growing popularity. "But it’s still really about the woman who’s wearing it and not about a label or a logo. It’s something you’ll reach for a couple times a week for years to come."

After so many women brought the "Deep End" home this year, AYR wants to give them more ways to wear the style. Matching sets will return in at least six colors in 2022, Winter said. Twists on the "Deep End" shirt include short sleeves, silk fabrications, and a shirtdress.

Is there really an appetite for that many? Current AYR fans would say yes.

Just before we spoke, Bonbrest, Winter, and their team were running a Manhattan photoshoot including the shirts when a self-proclaimed "Deep End" fan approached them.

"We had about six different colors in our hands," Bonbrest describes, "and [this woman] said, 'oh my God, I have three of those. It’s my favorite shirt.'"
