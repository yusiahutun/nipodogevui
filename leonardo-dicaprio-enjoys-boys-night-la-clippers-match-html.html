Leonardo DiCaprio enjoys a boys' night out at LA Clippers match

The noise generated by his connection to a teenage model was drowned out by a rather more welcoming sound as Leonardo DiCaprio made a courtside appearance at a Los Angeles Clippers game on Tuesday evening. 

The Oscar-winning actor, 48, was joined by rapper Q-Tip and backed by a raucous crowd at Crypto.com Arena, where they watched the home side take on San Francisco based rivals Golden State Warriors. 

DiCaprio's A-list appearance appeared to spur on the Clippers as they marched to a narrow 134-124 victory against the Warriors - whose losing streak now stretches to four consecutive games. 

The packed arena - formerly known as Staples Center - also welcomed Destiny's Child star Kelly Rowland, her husband Tim Weatherspoon and boxer Floyd Mayweather, Jr as the usual smattering of celebrity guests occupied front row seats.

DiCaprio's own appearance at the game followed a barrage of criticism for his connection to Israeli model Eden Polani - who at 19-years old age is 29-years his junior.

The actor - who has an undeniably rich history of dating younger women - was first pictured with Polani in January while attending Detroit-born singer Ebony Riley's release party for her new EP.

Seemingly missing from the event however was DiCaprio's current girlfriend Victoria Lamas, who at 23 is 25 years his junior and almost half his age.

But while he has since denied being romantically involved with the teenager, their appearance at the event has prompted  online criticism over what appears to be his preference for dating women aged 25 and younger. 

The uproar has reportedly resulted in the Israeli beauty deleting her Instagram account and returning home to her parents, while insiders have moved to clarify they are not romantically attached.
