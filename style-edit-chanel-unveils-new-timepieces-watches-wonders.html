Watches &amp; Wonders Edit: Chanel unveils new timepieces at the 2022 event, from the J12 Diamond Tourbi

Chanel roared out of the blocks with a dazzling range of new timepieces at this year’s Watches and Wonders – among them a new J12 showcasing a revolution in the maison’s watchmaking; new Mademoiselle pieces featuring Chanel’s iconic founder on the dial; and some scarlet, skeletonised versions of its much loved minimalist Boyfriend collection.

The instantly recognisable figure of the house’s founder, Gabrielle “Coco” Chanel, adorns the dial of three new pieces. The Mademoiselle J12 La Pausa shows her dressed in a Breton top and sailor trousers; on the J12 Gabrielle Calibre 3.1, she’s all in black and adorned with pearls; while the Mademoiselle J12. XS entwines her around the case as a figurine, depicted in white gold and black lacquer.

Inside Chanel’s Scotland-inspired finale for Paris Fashion Week 2022

The J12 Diamond Tourbillon Caliber 5 comes equipped with Chanel’s first in-house flying tourbillon movement, featuring a solitaire diamond in the centre of the tourbillon cage. It’s accompanied by further diamonds on the bezel, hands and crown, while the case comes in matt black ceramic and steel or glossy black ceramic and 18-carat white gold.

The clean lines of the Boyfriend collection, meanwhile, have been given the skeleton treatment on two new pieces. An evolution of the skeletonised Boyfriend first unveiled in 2018, the Boyfriend Skeleton Red Edition and Boyfriend Skeleton X-ray Red Edition, are both distinguished by the maison’s signature scarlet hue.

STYLE Edit: Chanel celebrates Bijoux de Diamants with the Allure Céleste

Those three weren’t the only highlights among the timepieces Chanel revealed at Watches and Wonders. Among numerous other new versions of the J12 was the first J12 33mm equipped with the self-winding calibre 12.2 movement; two new iterations of the J12 Baguette Diamond Bezel; the J12 Baguette Diamond Star 33mm, with baguette-cut diamonds smothering the bezel, dial and bracelet; the sharp ceramic J12 Black Star, equipped with the famed Caliber 12.1; the J12 X-ray Red Edition Rouge, a red tinted version of head turning transparent watch unveiled in 2020; new capsule collection Chanel Wanted, featuring iterations of six iconic Chanel models; and a new edition of the Mademoiselle Privé Bouton, part of the maison’s Métiers d’Art collection. Chanel’s novelties from Watches and Wonders this year include a work of art for every wrist.
