19 Corona Beer Nutrition Facts: Uncovering the Secrets of this Iconic Brew

Anyone familiar with Corona beer knows that this refreshing and iconic Mexican malt beverage has been delighting beer drinkers for decades. But what you may not have known is simply how much goes into each can or bottle of the popular brew. From carbohydrates to calories, saturated fats to proteins, we’re about to uncover all things Corona in regard to its nutritional facts. So buckle your seatbelts and get ready – we’ll be exploring 19 amazing nutrition facts about this Latin-style lager!

Calorie Content

A 12-ounce serving of Corona Extra contains about 149 calories. This is relatively low compared to many other beers, making it a lighter choice for those watching their calorie intake.

Alcohol By Volume (ABV)

Corona Extra has an ABV of 4.6%, which is about average for a lager. This means it contains less alcohol than heavier beers like stouts or IPAs.

Carbohydrates

Each 12-ounce bottle of Corona Extra contains 14 grams of carbohydrates. Most of these carbs come from the malted barley used in brewing.

Protein

Corona Beer contains less than a gram of protein per serving. This is fairly standard for beers, which are not typically a significant source of protein.

Fat Content

Corona Beer contains no fat. This is common for most beers, which usually get most of their calories from alcohol and carbohydrates.

Sodium

A bottle of Corona Extra contains about 14 mg of sodium. This is a relatively low amount, making it a decent option for those looking to limit their sodium intake.

Sugar

Corona Beer contains less than 1 gram of sugar per bottle. The small amount of sugar is naturally occurring from the brewing process.

Corona Light

Corona also offers a “light” version of its beer, Corona Light, which contains fewer calories (99 per serving) and less alcohol (3.9% ABV).

Corona Premier

Another lighter option from Corona is their Premier line, which has only 90 calories and 2.6 grams of carbs, making it one of the lowest carb beers on the market.

Gluten

Like most beers, Corona Beer contains gluten, as it’s made from malted barley. This means it’s not suitable for those with celiac disease or gluten sensitivity.

Non-Alcoholic Options

Corona offers non-alcoholic versions of their beers, providing an option for those who want to enjoy the taste of Corona without the alcohol.

Ingredients

Corona Beer is made from four basic ingredients: water, barley malt, corn, and hops. There are no additives or preservatives.

Preservative-Free

Corona Beer is free from preservatives. This aligns with the brand’s commitment to using natural ingredients in its brewing process.

Cholesterol

Like most beers, Corona is cholesterol-free. This makes it a heart-healthy choice when consumed in moderation.

Vitamins and Minerals

While Corona Beer is not a significant source of vitamins or minerals, it does contain trace amounts of nutrients like magnesium and potassium.

Fermentation Process

Corona Beer undergoes a fermentation process that uses yeast to convert the sugars in the malted barley into alcohol. This process also contributes to the beer’s flavor profile.

Flavor

Corona Beer is known for its light, crisp flavor with a hint of sweetness. This is due to the specific combination of barley, corn, and hops used in the brewing process.

Serving Suggestions

Corona Beer is often served with a wedge of lime or lemon, which enhances its flavor and makes for a refreshing drinking experience.

Pairing with Food

Corona pairs well with a variety of foods. Its light, crisp flavor complements spicy foods, grilled meats, and seafood particularly well. It’s the quintessential beer for a summer barbecue or a Mexican fiesta.

Conclusion

Corona beer has been a source of enjoyment for many people throughout the world. However, it’s important to remember that consuming any type of alcohol, especially beer, must be done in moderation. With such levels of carbohydrates and calories, consuming too much could take away from the joy that this popular beverage brings. As such, we hope this article helps you to know more about Corona so that you can make informed decisions when it comes to portion control. Ultimately, Corona is a great way to relax and kick back but only if drunk responsibly – and knowing 19 incredible facts on nutrition should help you do just that. Enjoy!
