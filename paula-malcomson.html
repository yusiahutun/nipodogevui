Early Life and career of Paula Malcomson

Paula Malcomson was born on 1st June 1970 in Belfast, Northern Ireland. She belongs to white ethnicity/race and holds a Northern Irish nationality.

Malcomson began her acting, appearing in 1992 dram, Another Girl Another Planet. The next year, she appeared as Allie Earp in American Western film, Tombstone, alongside Kurt Russell, Val Kilmer, Michael Biehn, and many more.

Malcomson then made appearances in the number of movies including, Dunston Checks In, Hamlet, A.I. Artificial Intelligence, The Hunger Games, The Hunger Games: Catching Fire, The Hunger Games: Mockingjay – Part 1, The Hunger Games: Mockingjay – Part 2 whereas her television credits include The Practice, Star Trek: Enterprise, Cold Case, and many more.

In 2007, Malcomson was nominated for Screen Actors Guild Awards for Outstanding Performance by an Ensemble in a Drama Series, Deadwood. Similarly, she was also nominated as Best Actress in a Supporting Role in Drama Ray Donovan for Irish Film and Television Awards.

Malcomson’s most recent screen appearance was in 2018 TV series, Come Home, alongside Anthony Boyle, Brandon Brownlee, Christopher Eccleston, and many others.
