DJ Fatxo Comments on Jeff Mwathi's Death, Says He Doesn't Know How It Happened

  • While making a comeback on social media after a while, DJ Fatxo has offered to give his truth, though in bits, in respect to Jeff Mwathi's mysterious death
  • Born Lawrence Njuguna, Fatxo insists that he did not partake in killing the 23-year-old who was in his (Fatxo's) house on the fateful night he met his tragic end
  • The Mugithi-singing artiste said he would soon give his side of the story in full; the Director of Public Prosecutions (DPP) Noordin Haji is awaited to determine the next step

Nairobi - Popular Kikuyu benga singer Lawrence Njuguna has absolved himself of any wrongdoing as regards Geoffrey Mwathi's death, even as the Director of Public Prosecutions (DPP) Noordin Haji is awaited to give further directions on the matter.

The artiste who has DJ Fatxo as an alias was the big cheese in a trio of persons of interest in the detectives' probe into Mwathi's mysterious death.

It is in Fatxo's house that the 23-year-old budding interior decorator met his tragic end on February 22.

DJ Fatxo: I'm innocent

While it was primarily believed that Mwathi took his own life, it later emerged that the young man might have been murdered after all.

Commenting on the matter after a while, Fatxo said he does not know how Mwathi died, seeming to dismiss the belief that he had a hand, or got an idea of how the deceased met his end.

This was after a Kenyan identified as Dube Namasaka asked for the entertainer's comment;

"But how did it happen? Can it be told to clear the air DJ?" asked Namasaka.

Short of words, Fatxo said;

"I also don't know, but wait for my version. soon"

On the same platform, Fatxo revealed how being linked with Mwathi's death took a toll on him and his family, their matriarch being the worst hit by it.

Fatxo was commenting on a post by lawyer Wahome Thuku who revealed that he had had a chat with him through a phone call.

"I read this loudly for my mother to gather some strength and hope for tomorrow. She said God bless you. It has been hell. I will meet you soon, God willing and tell you how life feels when you're denied freedom of speech and freedom of movement for something you never did and something you would never do even in another world. Bless you, Wahome," he said.

DCI 'absolves' DJ Fatxo and his clique

Meanwhile, Fatxo and his clique of two other men got a reprieve after the investigators attached to the Directorate of Criminal Investigations (DCI) could not get the x-factor to link them with Mwathi's death.

After reconstructing the scene and examining all the crucial elements, the detectives concluded that the trio could not be pinned down as the perpetrators of the young man's supposed murder.

The file is now with the DPP, who is expected to determine the next course of action.
