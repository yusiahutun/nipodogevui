20+ modern gate designs in Kenya you can take inspiration from

A sturdy gate design secured with a compound wall ensures outsiders and thieves cannot easily access your property. The best gate designs fit the property structure and style, the owner's personality and needs, and complement its surroundings. So, what are modern gate designs in Kenya you can take inspiration from?

Modern gate designs in Kenya

There are numerous simple gate designs in Kenya. You can acquire any of your choices depending on your purpose and budget. Kenyan gates are mainly made of wood, steel, aluminium, and iron. Here are some of the gate designs you can take inspiration from.

1. Double swing gate

The double swing gate is a popular choice when enough space is available for entry. Its double-leaf design allows its opening angle of up to 180°. Its standard widths and heights ensure pedestrians and vehicles can pass through easily.

2. Wooden gate designs

Luxury-looking wooden gates will automatically give your property a classy makeover. Their designs are typically ordinary, easy to build and affordable since they are made from panels of shaded wood. Wooded gates are known to be maintenance-free and anti-termites.

3. Sliding gate designs

The sliding gate model works in a telescopic movement, where several small sliding panels move one after the other to open or close. You can choose from a manual sliding gate design or automated systems that work on a motor and a transmitter that controls the gate movements.

Sliding gates can be constructed from various materials, including steel, aluminium, wood, and polyvinyl chloride (PVC).

4. Folding gate design

These gates are also called retractable gates and work well as space separators in outdoor areas. They are made of multiple doors, attached using hinges, and each panel folds into the other to make way. Due to increased demand for these gates, designers are devising new patterns and colour themes to blend with the rest of the exterior décor.

5. Single swing gate

A single swing gate has one gate panel attached to a single post, opening and closing on hinges. If you are on a limited budget, this would be the most affordable alternative since they need half the hardware that a double-swing gate does. Single-leaf gate openers are designed for gates with a single leaf, and the maximum gate size is typically around 20 feet.

6. Entrance gate

An entrance gate is installed at the entry point of the house. Depending on the entrance size and the purpose to be met, there are several Entrance gates you can choose from in Kenya. Choosing the right material for your Entrance gate is also key. Metal gates are the best bet for low maintenance and durability. Wood may look classy but can be quite expensive.

7. Outdoor gate

An outdoor gate always comes outside the living area and is typically installed on the exit or entry points. It can be made from wood, steel, aluminium or iron. These gates shield your house from direct views and let you enjoy privacy. They also act as the first line of defence for your property and prevent unwanted entries.

8. Steel gate design

Steel is an anti-corrosive material which lasts for quite a long time. The mirror-like shine of this gate can instantly draw the attention of the onlookers to your property. There are several steel gate designs in the Kenyan market, such as steel main gate designs for homes, steel sliding gates, steel fence gates, and fancy steel gate designs.

9. Iron gate designs

Iron is difficult to break through, durable and easy to maintain; therefore, using an iron gate for your entry point to your property or home is ideal. Some of the iron gate designs include fancy iron gates, decorative iron gates, and cast iron gate designs. These Kenyan gates are designed in a way that they are vandal-proof, eco-friendly and rot-proof.

10. Wrought iron gates

Wrought iron gates are your best choice if you need a stylish, long-lasting and break-proof gate. Wrought iron is a treated iron material with lower carbon content than raw iron. Because of its anti-corrosive and weather-resistant properties, it has become Kenya's best material for exterior gates. Classic metallic gates made of wrought iron are suitable to be installed as driveway gates.

11. Aluminium gate designs

Aluminium gates rarely rust even when exposed to saltwater; hence, they are suitable for homes in a corrosive environment. Also, aluminium is lightweight and easily moulded into creative designs. Despite its delicate looks, the aluminium gate is quite strong and hard to destroy.

12 Automatic gate designs

Automatic gate designs are fitted with the power of an automated system that can sense a movement and trigger the motor. It opens up wide enough for people to pass in and closes by itself.

Many property owners are now choosing automatic gate designs for maximum protection. Swing and sliding automatic gates are Kenya's main types of automatic gates.

13. School gate design

School gate designs play important roles, like restricting access to unauthorised personnel and maintaining the internal discipline of learners in academic institutions. These gates are always designed in a way that they can resist environmental challenges to safeguard the students and staff.

The gates must also conform to the National School Safety Council's standards to prevent potential internal and external threats.

14. Security gate design

Security gates are normally designed to restrict access to residential, commercial, and industrial facilities. They are usually installed on the fencing areas or driveways of a property. As a result of the vertical arrangement of the panels, it's hard to climb on them. Usually, the bars also end up like sharp spikes to threaten potential trespassers.

15. Fence gate designs

Fence gate designs are common in large towns and are always recommended in areas with minimal security issues. The antique-style wooden gate is a refreshing change from the conventional metal gates.

This type of gate is designed as a barrier attached to the compound, which can be opened or closed. It can be automated or operated manually.

16. Industrial gate design

These gates are appropriate for places frequented by many people, like supermarkets, hospitals, and public offices. Industrial gates are designed from robust materials such as steel, iron, or aluminium. They are engineered to withstand the test of time and weather. Their towering presence commands attention, exuding a sense of authority and reliability.

17. A wooden double gate

A wooden double gate provides solid barriers that protect you from prying eyes. They are also relatively inexpensive compared to gates made from steel or iron. The above gate works as a swing style and opens inward to let you in. It can withstand strong currents when secured with iron hinges.

18. Custom gates

Custom gates are designed according to the client's specific requirements, including a completely one-off design, intricate patterns or a traditional look. The design is made exclusively for the client and cannot be found elsewhere. They can be made from all kinds of materials.

19. Ornamental gate design

These gates often showcase intricate scrollwork, filigree details, and ornate embellishments. The ornamental gate design is made of aluminium or steel. Indeed, classically inspired ornamental gates exude an air of luxury and evoke the past with their old-world charm.

20. Manual swing barrier gate

They are normally designed using strong steel and are suitable for unattended zones with low traffic numbers. This gate usually has an extra latch post and padlock for added security. The manual swing barrier gate is available as a single or double-leaf version in either a standard or heavy-duty box section.

Frequently asked questions

  • How are Kenyan gates operated? Kenyan gate operations are either automated or manual.
  • What are the strongest gate designs in Kenya? The strongest gate designs in Kenya are those made of steel. Steel can leverage forces associated with gate operations more than iron or aluminium.
  • What are the different types of gates in Kenya? The most common types of gates in Kenya are swing, slide, and automatic gates.
  • How much does an automatic gate cost in Kenya? An automatic gate in Kenya costs approximately Kshs 130 thousand for a swing gate and Kshs 110 thousand for a sliding gate.
  • What is the disadvantage of an automatic gate? The automatic gate is expensive, its installation requires a professional, and it also needs maintenance. Additionally, their operation relies on electricity.
  • How long do Aluminium gates last? Aluminium gates can last more than 10 to 20 years if properly maintained.
