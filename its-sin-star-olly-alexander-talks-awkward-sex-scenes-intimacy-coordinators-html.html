It's A Sin: Olly Alexander talks about 'awkward' sex scenes

He's currently impressing viewers with his performance on groundbreaking Channel 4 series It's A Sin about the rise of HIV in the 1980s.

And Olly Alexander has revealed how the cast and crew of the drama handled the 'awkward' sex scenes that take place on the show.

'It is awkward, but the thing was, on the show we had people called intimacy coordinators and their jobs, they’re amazing, they’re jobs are to help with the sex scenes and everyone doing the sex scenes to feel safe and fine and not awkward,' he told Capital's Jimmy Hill on Monday evening.

'Suddenly you're naked and moaning and thrusting!' It's A Sin star Olly Alexander has talked about the show's 'awkward' sex scenes... but revealed 'intimacy coordinators' made the cast feel 'safe'

'In the past you’d say in the stage directions “they had sex” and you’d come on stage and figure it out and say “oh what are we going to do!”, so many things can go wrong.

'So we have these intimacy coordinators and they really take you step by step through the process, they make you feel really comfortable, they’re always there to say, “in this shot, you’re seeing this of your body” and if you’re not seeing something they’ll throw you a dressing gown to cover it, so you’re not naked for 12 hours.
