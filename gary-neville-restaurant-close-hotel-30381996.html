Restaurant inside Gary Neville's hotel shuts down after just four months

A restaurant inside Gary Neville's Stock Exchange Hotel has been forced to close its doors after just four months of business.

Replacing Tom Kerridge's Bull and Bear in Manchester, Stock Market Grill opened to much fanfare at the beginning of March.But the eatery, owned by brothers Joe and Daniel Schofield with wine expert James Brandwood, has now ceased operations.

The trio are the brains behind Manchester's award-winning Schofield's Bar and were already familiar to Neville due to owning the hotel’s cocktail bar Sterling, which will continue to trade interrupted.

A statement released by the Stock Exchange Hotel reads: "Stock Exchange Hotel have taken the decision to close the doors to STOCK MARKET GRILL, as of 3rd July 23.

"The restaurant will, however, continue to serve breakfast and in-room dining to all overnight guests. In the meantime, the focus will be on STERLING, the premium cocktail bar located in The Vault at the Stock Exchange Hotel. The team are continuing to develop the STERLING concept and it’s offering."

Neville announced the opening of a new restaurant inside the Stock Exchange Hotel at the end of 2022. He said at the time: "I am incredibly excited to be working with Joe, Daniel and James again on the launch of The Stock Market Grill. I know they will bring another world class-brand to the Stock Exchange Hotel and to our city."

The Manchester United legend rakes in tens of millions of pounds every year thanks to his property development empire. Neville established investment company 'Relentless' in 2015 and he also runs 'Zerum', a property and construction consultancy business.

The ex-defender has been involved in property development since his playing days with a vast realm consisting of The Stock Exchange Hotel, Hotel Football, a luxury hotel overlooking Old Trafford, and the St Michael's project, a £120m redevelopment plan which aims to build hotels, offices, apartments and a high-end restaurant in the city centre.
