The 13 Best Nail Wraps and Stickers of 2024

Let’s be honest: Painting your nails at home can be a challenge. While super handy tools (like the Olive & June The Poppy Manicure Tool) make it a touch easier to paint with your non-dominant hand, it’s still all too easy to accidentally get a smidge of polish outside the lines. Thankfully, nail wraps and nail stickers exist to make perfecting at-home manicures easier than ever. 

“Nail wraps are perfect for at-home DIY manicures,” says celebrity manicurist Pattie Yankee, who is a consultant for Dashing Diva. “They are already dry and ‘cured,’ so [there’s] no chance of messing up a nail if you need to get a phone call or attend to your child. You can stop the application process at any point in between without compromising the results, which is impossible with other nail coatings.”

We’re all for making DIY manicures as simple as possible, so we set out to find the best nail stickers and wraps of 2024. With the help of Yankee (and four other manicurists) we narrowed down the over-saturated nail wrap and sticker market to just 13 picks that offer countless designs and salon-quality results — all from the comfort of your home.

Best Overall

Color Street Nail Strips

Color Street

What We Love: There’s a wide variety of patterns, colors, and finishes to choose from — and many are sold in a variety of sizes. 

What We Don’t Love: The strips are very sticky, so you need to put them on carefully because they’re difficult to adjust once placed. 

Professional manicurist and Nailing Hollywood CEO, Mazz Hanna, can’t recommend Color Street Nail Polish Strips enough. The ultra-popular strips are sold in sets of 16 and are designed to be applied directly to the nail — no need for any extra adhesive or a UV light to cure. 

What really sets these strips apart, though, is the fact that there are hundreds of designs to choose from, many of which are sold in standard and petite sizes, so they cater to a variety of nail bed shapes. Right now, some of our favorites include Grow on You, a clear sticker with delicate floral designs, and Waterfall’s Edge, a sparkly pastel wrap with fun geometric designs. 

Though the strips are relatively easy to apply (simply peel and stick!), know that the adhesive is very sticky, so you’ll want to precisely place them as they can be difficult to adjust once on the nail bed. 

Designs: 100+ | Number Per Pack: 16 | Adhesive Type: Stick-on | Longevity: Up to 10 days

Best Budget

Olive & June Nail Art Stickers

Olive & June

What We Love: These stickers are sold in a bunch of cute themed packs, with flowers, fruit, stars, and other designs to choose from. 

What We Don’t Love: They don’t wear well without a topcoat, as the edges get snagged on things if not sealed down. 

Celebrity manicurist Marisa Carmichael loves the Olive & June Nail Art Stickers because of how easy they are to use, and how they instantly update any DIY nail look. The stickers, which are sold in over 20 themed packs (including simplistic minimalist designs and kitschy snack foods), simply need to be pressed on and sealed with a top coat for up to seven days of seamless wear.  

Designs: 23 | Number Per Pack: 36 | Adhesive Type: Stick-on | Longevity: Up to 7 Days

Lauren B. Beauty

Sold in 14 designs — including holographic and glittery options – each pack includes 18 gel nail wraps that simply need to be stuck on and filed down to fit. 

Designs: 14 | Number Per Pack: 18 | Adhesive Type: Stick-on | Longevity: Up to 14 Days

Best on Amazon

Amazon

What We Don’t Love: Not all of the decals are stickers, so some require more patience and precision to apply.

Looking for near-instant gratification with your nail sticker purchase? Designs: 3 | Number Per Pack: 12 Sheets of stickers and five boxes of sequins | Adhesive Type: Stick-on and nail glue | Longevity: Up to 10 Days

What to Keep in Mind

Design

In terms of design, both nail wraps and stickers come in hundreds of designs, including trendy patterns, intricate nail art, and even simplistic solid colors. The design you ultimately choose depends on your personal preference. For example, if you like your manicure to make a statement, opt for a bold design: Sally Hansen Salon Effects Real Nail Polish Strips has some of the most eclectic options. On the other hand, if you prefer solid colors, the Paintbucket Nail Wrap Kit offers a variety of richly-hued solids. And when you want just a small statement decal, go for mini nail stickers, like Deco Miami Nail Stickers.

It’s also important to think about the design, or rather the functionality of the different types of nail wraps, though. “Gel wraps are thicker and give more protection to the nail as well as giving them a plump gel polish finish,” Yankee says. “Wraps made of plastic or vinyl are medium thickness, while those composed of polish are the thinnest, offering the least strength and protection to your nails.” Gel wraps are especially great for weak, brittle nails as they serve as a protective barrier. Dashing Diva Glaze Strips are our favorite. 

Stickers per Pack

Point blank: The more stickers per pack, the better chance you’ll have of creating the nail art idea you have in mind. For wraps, the greater number per pack, the easier it will be to find a size that most closely matches your nail bed shape. GellyDrops Semi-Curel Gel Nail Strips come with 34, the highest number on our list. 

Size

While Mani Me offers custom nail wraps which makes sizing a no-brainer, pretty much every other nail wrap brand sells in standard and petite sizing (with petite silhouettes often geared toward kids). That said, Yankee explains that the best way to determine sizing for your nails is by trial and error — just as with press-on nails. “Most strips are on a sheet, though, so you can ‘hover’ the sheet over each nail, which will help you easily select the proper size,” she explains. The trick when sizing is to make sure the wrap isn’t too wide.  “If it overlaps onto the skin, it will lift prematurely,” she adds, noting that it’s often best to choose the smaller nail wrap if you’re in between sizes.

Your Questions, Answered

What is the difference between nail wraps and stickers?

“The difference between nail stickers and wraps is the fact that one offers a full nail coverage and the other is just a sticker focused on decoration,” says Nailing Hollywood Artist, Morgan Dixon. “Nail wraps are used to provide the full nail look and stickers provide more specific nail art you can apply over polish.”

Traditional, in-salon nail wraps also exist (but they’re not typically recommended for at-home use). “Traditional nail wraps are technically referring to a fabric coating such as silk, linen, or fiberglass strips cut into shapes and applied to the nail with an adhesive,” Yankee shares.

How do you apply and remove nail wraps and stickers?

Though each nail wrap product comes with its own set of instructions, Carmichael has a method that she swears by: “Clean the nail to remove any oils with acetone, apply a base coat, and let it completely dry,” she says. From there, find your perfect fit by hovering the strips over top of your nails. Once you’ve selected your strips, peel off the back and press them into place as close to your cuticles as possible. Fold the excess over the free edge of the nail, file it off, and finish with top coat. For gel nail strips, you’ll need to cure them with a UV lamp, too. 

The application process for nail stickers compared to nail wraps is a bit less intensive: as all you have to do is place stickers with a pair of tweezers (for precision, says Carmichael) and seal with a top coat. 

The removal process for both is relatively easy — nail polish remover or rubbing alcohol should do the trick. If you’re removing gel nail strips, soak your nails as you usually would post-gel to avoid causing any damage.

How long do nail wraps last?

According to Carmichael, individually-placed accent stickers typically last for between seven and 10 days. Meanwhile, nail wraps can last up to two weeks depending on the quality of the products and how they were applied, Yankee says.

Are nail wraps and stickers bad for your nails?

So long as you apply them and remove them with care, no. “If you peel them off, they will likely strip up layers of your nail with them,” Stone warns. “Proper removal is necessary with any nail design and that includes stickers and wraps.”
