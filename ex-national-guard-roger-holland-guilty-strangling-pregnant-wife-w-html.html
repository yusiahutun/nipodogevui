Guilty: Roger Holland was convicted of murdering his wife and their unborn child

A former member of the National Guard has been found guilty of murdering his pregnant wife at their Minnesota home earlier this year.

Roger Holland was convicted of four murder charges relating to the death of his wife Margorie, and their unborn child.

The 37-year-old was found at the bottom of a staircase in the couple's home in March. A coroner later ruled she had been strangled.

Holland, 37, did not visibly react to the verdict when it was read at 1am on Tuesday,  Twin Cities reported.

He is due back in court on Tuesday afternoon for sentencing, and could face life in prison without parole.

'We are pleased to have brought Roger Holland to justice for this senseless and violent crime that took the lives of Margorie Holland and her unborn child,' attorney James Backstrom said in a statement.

The retired Texas National Guard member, who had served in Iraq and Afghanistan, had maintained throughout that he didn't hurt his wife, who was nearly four months pregnant.

A medical examiner said however that she had been strangled, and prosecutors said Holland had scratches on his head that were evidence of a struggle.

Prosecutor Phil Prokopowicz added that the couple's two-year marriage was disintegrating and the couple had been under financial hardship.

Mrs Holland had just found out the couple were $160,000 in debt, and had threatened to leave her husband just before she was found dead, Kare11 reported.

A text message sent the night before the murder read: 'the only thing she could do was divorce him,' the prosecutor said.

Searches about whether it was possible to break your neck while falling down stairs were also found on Holland's laptop.

Holland told police he left the apartment to get breakfast for himself and his wife and when he returned he found her face-down on the floor, wrapped in a blanket and unconscious.

The couple had met while working for the National Guard, but moved to Minnesota when they discovered they were having a baby, so Mrs Holland could be nearer her family.

With the murder charge relating to Holland's unborn child, the jury didn't have to find intent that he wanted to harm the fetus, only that he intended to kill his wife.

The conviction will be appealed automatically under Minnesota law.
