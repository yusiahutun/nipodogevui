Mama Dora Nyambe: 29-Year-Old Single Mum Proudly Takes Care of 15 Children

  • A single woman watching over 15 kids says she is proud of her role and the path she has decided to take
  • Dora Moono Nyambe is an adoptive mother of 12 and fosters three more children in a village in Zambia
  • Despite facing disapproval from the villagers, the 29-year-old has showered love to the village by building a school

Dora Moono Nyambe is a 29-year-old single mother who has 15 children under her care and watches over them by herself.

She became an online sensation when she shared videos of her life as a mum-of-15 on social media.

She was on her way to China to start her career as a teacher when she visited the village of Mapapa in Zambia.

Upon arrival, Nyambe saw the poverty in the land and took action by building a learning institution.

The single mom took 15 children to her home and shared her motherhood experience on Tiktok, where she became famous.

Opposition from villagers

The Sun reported she faced lots of opposition from people because of her unmarried state and age.

"When I started to adopt, people said I couldn't do it because of my age and being unmarried.I am happy to say you were all wrong. I am a proud adoptive mother of 12 and fostering three."

Prove people wrong

People's disapproval did not stop Nyambe, and she hired security guards, fearing that her life was at risk.

She also plans to build a proper medical clinic in the village.

Currently, the village clinic is a small, poorly equipped shack. What few supplies it has are frequently and easily stolen."The children who come to us are often in need of urgent medical care, some diagnosed with chronic illnesses. Without a proper, well-stocked clinic the children are unable to receive the care they so desperately need," she said.

Single mother has nowhere to sleep

In related news, TUKO.co.ke recently reported about a single mother who was supported by Kenyans to find a place to sleep.

The woman had appealed to Kenyans via a Facebook parenting group to help her find accommodation for her and her baby as they went to bury their baby daddy in Kisii.

She was able to find a place to lay her head five days after the post at a prestigious Bnb with a fully-stocked fridge with food.
