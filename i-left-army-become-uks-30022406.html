'I left Army to become UKs most glam lorry driver rude men dont bother me'

A glamorous lorry driver is encouraging more women to apply for jobs in the haulage industry – but warned them that they would need thick skins.

Nicolle – who calls herself ‘Trucker Baddie’ – has endured misogynistic comments from blokes who have belittled her for thriving in a male dominated field.

But the trucker, from Sunderland, is strong willed having served seven years in the army after joining at 16.

She was eventually medically discharged before her boyfriend Ben, who is also a HGV driver, inspired her to consider a career switch.

The 28-year-old had no qualifications after leaving school so gave it a go having obtained her licence in the military – and the decision completely changed her life.

Speaking to Daily Star, Nicolle, who has 154,000 TikTok followers, said: “I have always said this and tried to use my social media to encourage younger girls to get into HGV driving.

“You have got to have a thick skin though because you do get a lot of comments from men. I am not labelling every man in the industry because you get a lot of lovely people.

“But I have had men following me with their phones taking videos and pictures without asking. I have also had comments saying ‘what are you doing this is a man’s job’ and ‘you shouldn’t be driving a truck’.

“It is simple things like because I have always used a curtainsider, I have had men approach me saying ‘you will need a hand with those curtains’ and they just try and interfere with my job. It is just little things like that but I have had a lot worse.

“I’ve always had quite thick skin and nothing really bothers me. I laugh at them and walk away.”

Nicolle initially wasn’t taken by the job and said it took time getting used to manoevuering a trailer but she quickly “fell in love” with it.

She described being obsessed with the freedom of being out on the road, especially in the sun, without anybody harassing her.

Her boyfriend also felt the same way but the pair spent years working 12 to 15 hour days and they were barely getting to see one another or their families.

By this point Nicolle had gained a large following on Instagram for being a female trucker and the couple thought of ways they could capitalise on it.

And they eventually joined OnlyFans to give them a better work life balance.

Ben, 32, who met Nicolle via Tinder eight years ago, told us: ““Nicolle found her niche as a trucker girl who also does OnlyFans, as girls who drive trucks get a lot of male attention.

“So she thought ‘why not let them see more’.”

Despite now being successful content creators, the passion for HGV driving never went away.

And although she doesn’t need to financially, Nicolle still works for an agency and drives lorries sporadically.

She said: ”For me I absolutely love getting out on the road. I get bored being at home doing the same thing and it is having that break with the truck and I love the trucking and being out on my own.

“I have been a worker from a young age so working from home, I do love it, don't get me wrong, but we have the freedom to do what we want and I love that but it is nice to get out to work.”

As for her success on OnlyFans, she added: “I would never have thought that from the age of 27 I would not have to go out to work… I would never have thought to have the freedom at this age.”
