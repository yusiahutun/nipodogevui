Nine utterly bizarre man of the match awards from fish to bag of spuds and cash prize

Erling Haaland's modest sum is not the first bizarre man of the match award to be handed out after a fixture.

Bottles of champagne have gone out of fashion in recent years with cash prizes, food and even mobile data leaving players baffled while posing for snaps after games.

Manchester City's Treble winning hero scored twice in their 5-3 win over Yokohama F. Marinos in Tokyo and was handed a cheque of 1,000,000 in Japanese Yen - which works out as £5,505. A lot of money to most people, but not perhaps the Norwegian goal machine, who is said to earn a basic salary of £375,000-per-week - before bonuses.

Here, Daily Star Sport has taken a look at eight more of the weirdest awards to be handed out at games - including a big plate of fish.

A live chicken

In 2019, Nyasa Big Bullets striker Hassan Kajoke was handed a live chicken after his fine display against Karonga United.

The player scored a brace as his side thumped their opposition 5-0 - but he could not hide the shock on his face as one supporter gave him a small cardboard box which was inscribed 'Kajoke BB'.

Red stripes had been painted on the chicken, matching the side’s kit. "You just got to love a Bullets supporter." Bullets said on their social media account.

A bag of spuds

Gaelic football club Wicklow presented their hurler Daniel Staunton with a prized bag of potatoes after his performance in a GAA match.

The image of the player collecting his prized token went viral on social media. One wrote in response: “We won a bag of coal at pub quiz in Donegal one night!”

Another stated: “Finally!! A proper MOTM prize.” A third wrote: “Has this just suddenly become a thing at club level? I’m desperate for a thread of the most random ones..scrap the €50 vouchers!

"Show me the locally made pots of jam, a sliced pan and more spuds. Always more spuds.”

A bike

Aberdeen's Stephen Glass won bike for being man of the match at the 1995 League Cup final against Dundee.

The glamorous mountain bike, which was decked out in Coca-Cola colours, was there for all to see as the then-teenager put on a wry smile for the cameras.

Some mobile data

Mamelodi Sundowns skipper Hlompho Kekana’s brilliant display warranted him the award of a 5GB chunk of mobile data.

In what is likely a first for football, the South African organisation gave the player some free roaming data.

Though it might seem a bit off-the-cuff a first - the country is known to have pretty high rates when it comes to phone charges as it is a valued possession.

A fish

When Arsenal star Martin Odegaard was at Real Sociedad, he won the Player of the Month award for the club, and to reward his efforts he was given a massive fish.

The Norwegian was on loan at the Basque club and scored twice in his first four La Liga appearances.

Indeed, no one denied he wasn’t a deserved winner, just that the prize he was handed was a bit of an odd choice.

A video game

In 2020, Said Benrahma received the new Crash Bandicoot game for his man of the match performance against neighbours Fulham in the Carabao Cup.

The then-Brentford player posed with a beaming grin after The Bees defeated Scott Parker’s men 3-0 in the competition.

A Burger King hat

River Plate goalkeeper Franco Armani pulled out all the stops - and all he was awarded was a Burger King hat. The then-31-year-old smiled as cameras flashed on him wearing his new garment back in 2018.

Someone wrote on Twitter: "It appears to be made of metal rather than paper, but even so that is the crappest award I've ever seen. The TV folk made him put it on and everything"

Another said: “River's Franco Armani was crowned, quite literally, Man of the Match by sponsors Burger King last night. Modern football."

An enormous credit card

Kaiser Chiefs midfielder George Maluleka was given an oversized credit card by ABSA bank in 2014.

With the card came a wad of money too, in fairness.

And it’s a good thing as well - it's a high chance he would be to fit that thing into an ATM machine while paying contactless would require a fair bit of graft.
