How Much Is Ivan Shaw Net Worth?

Ivan Shaw, who is now starring in the popular NBC show, actually did not want to become an actor while he was young. In fact, Ivan did not take any interest in acting until college.

While Ivan was attending UCLA, he felt a love for acting for the first time. Upon realizing that acting was his passion, Ivan told his dad that he wanted to drop out of college, which he obviously rejected.

So, Ivan made a ridiculous deal with his father that he would finish his education if his dad would let him do whatever he wanted after college. Ivan begrudgingly finished school with a degree in math, economics, and computer science.

After graduating from UCLA, he moved to New York for a contract role on All My Children. During this time, he also worked in various Off-Broadway productions.

Following a series of appearances on TV shows such as Haunted, CSI: NY, Charmed, The Closer, Monk, The Mind of the Married Man, All My Children, General Hospital, The Young and the Restless, and Baywatch Hawaii, Ivan landed the role of Adam Webster in primetime NBC series The Book of Daniel in 2006.

Following the series, Ivan began writing and directing short movies, music videos, and commercials while appearing in TV shows like On The Verge, Casual, New Girl, Insecure, Better Things, Silicon Valley, Rizzoli & Isles, NCIS, CSI, Monk, and The Closer.

The Chicago Med actor has also appeared in plenty of feature films. Some of them are Get Him to the Greek, L!fe Happens, The Truth About Angels, and Rush Hour 2. He also starred in Bertha Bay-Sa Pan’s feature film, Almost Perfect (2011) opposite Kelly Hu.

Recently, Ivan was also part of two projects, The Cleaning Lady and That Dirty Black Bag. Ivan played Marco De La Rosa in The Cleaning Lady, husband to the cleaning lady and father to their son, Luca. In the show, his gambling problem often gets in the way of family responsibilities. Whereas, in That Dirty Black Bag, Ivan played the character of Kurt.

Furthermore, Ivan has associate produced a film called The Time Being, which premiered at the 2012 Toronto International Film Festival. He has also written a short film named The Tip, directed by Tyler Brooks.

The next project that Ivan is set to appear in is an Apple show called Dear Edward, which is based on the novel by Ann Napolitano. The show is about a plane crash where the only survivor is a 12-year-old boy, Edward.
