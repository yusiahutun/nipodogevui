2 coin selling for THOUSANDS and you could have one

This isn’t about finding a James Bond fiver or checking for one of the AK47 notes either.

This time the big money is going on a £2 with a very specific printing error.

The coin in question was released in 2007 by the Royal Mint to mark the 200th anniversary of the 1807 Abolition Of The Slave Trade.

There were 8.5million of them minted.

But, the good news for collectors is that some of them were made with an error.

That means they are given a scarcity 1 grading, which means that they’re selling for more than they’re worth.

If you can’t quite see the error it’s because it’s not on the front or back.

In fact it’s on the side of the coin in the engraving which says “Am I Not A Man And Brother”.

As normal, when the queen's head is facing upwards the writing should run along the side so you can read it.

But it seems some of the coins were printed with the writing upside down.

It might not sound like a big deal, but these rare coins are exchanging hands for big bucks online.

One plucky seller is trying their luck by listing the coin for a whopping £100,000.

And the listing is proving popular, with 98 people watching the ultra rare coin.

The seller wrote: “Circulated coin in very good condition.

“This comes with proof of error.”

Another seller is listing the coin for a modest £800.

Another is being sold for £199 and one more for £39.99.

A quick look at the coins that have sold in recent months reveal they normally go for around £240.

But one lucky seller was able to cash in, selling one for £800.
