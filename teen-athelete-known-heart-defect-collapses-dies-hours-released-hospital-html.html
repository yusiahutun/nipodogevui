Naperville Central athlete Matt Skowronski dies after leaving hospital

Died: Matt Skowronski, 16, died just hours after visiting the hospital concerning a heart condition

A 16-year-old boy with a heart defect died just hours after being seen by doctors in Illinois.  

Matt Skowronski was diagnosed with an enlarged aorta in sixth grade. The condition eventually led to his death at the weekend.

'He lived everyday to the fullest,' dad Marc Skowronski said about his son to the Chicago Tribune. 'He was just a person who was always smiling and always happy.' 

The teenager led an active lifestyle despite having to endure a life-threatening condition and would often be seen playing volleyball, basketball and hanging out with friends and family 

'I know he is going to be greatly missed by his family, friends, coaches and teammates,' his father said.

'The outpouring of support from the community has been unbelievable. We never realized Matt touched that many peoples' lives in so many different ways.' 

Matt even played varsity basketball and volleyball at Naperville Central.

Team player: Matt wore No. 15 on the Redhawks volleyball team and he was respected and well-liked. At 6 feet 9 inches tall as a 16-year-old, Matt always had towered over his peers

Family man: Matt Skowronski with his grandmothers including Georgeann Meyers Skowronski (see on the left in the first picture and on the right in the second)

At 6 feet 8 inches tall, he was a center in basketball and middle blocker in volleyball.

'He always had a smile on his face,' varsity volleyball coach Roger Strausberger said to the Tribune. 'He loved playing the sport and loved being around the guys on the team.'

Matt quickly made friends among his teammates because he was 'cheerful' with a 'great personality.' 

On the school's basketball team, Matt wore No. 54 and played on varsity for about a month this season while others were injured before returning to play with the sophomores.

On Monday night, the Illinois High School Volleyball Coaches Association held their boys volleyball Senior All-Star game. 

One of Matt's teammate, Jack Burton, was chosen to play and wore Matt's No. 15 jersey. 

More than $1,500 was raised from tickets sales and given to the Skowronski family.  

Throughout his short life, Matt didn't experience any serious issue with his heart until his very last day.

Best buddies: Teammate, Jack Bruton (left), spoke highly of the 16-year-old Naperville basketball player: 'He was such a good player and had such a bright future'

He had request visits to a cardiologist twice or three times a year along with medication that he took twice a day. Incredibly, it never prevented him from playing the games he loved.

He wasn't feeling well on Saturday morning, so his mother, Kelly, took him to the hospital to see his doctors and have some tests done.

The cardiologist said Matt's aorta had enlarged slightly more than before, but that was to be expected. Matt underwent numerous tests, but after eight hours, doctors sent him home. 

At one point, Matt got up to grab a pop from the fridge, and his friends saw him collapse.

He spent his final minutes surrounded by his family and friends.

'At least he was home and we got to be with him in the last half-hour,' his father said to the Daily Herald. 'We were able to be there for him.' 

His coaches, along with family and friends, plan to make a permanent memorial for him in the honor garden at Naperville Central.
