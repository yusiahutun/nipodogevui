Meaning of Is It Really So Strange? by The Smiths

The Smiths' song "Is It Really So Strange?" is a captivating composition that delves into the complexities of love, resilience, and the overwhelming power of emotions. Through its poignant lyrics and striking melodies, the song explores various themes that resonate with listeners on a deep emotional level.One standout lyric in the song is, "I left the North, I traveled South." This lyric symbolizes the protagonist's journey of self-discovery and transformation. It speaks of leaving behind familiar surroundings and venturing into the unknown. This theme can be extended to represent the courage to step outside of one's comfort zone and embrace change.Another powerful line is, "Oh yes, you can kick me, and you can punch me, and you can break my face, but you won't change the way I feel, 'cause I love you." This lyric explores the idea of unwavering devotion and the resilience of love. It suggests that despite facing adversity or mistreatment, love remains steadfast. This theme can be interpreted as a commentary on the often complicated and tumultuous nature of relationships, where love can endure even in the face of abuse.The chorus of the song also provides thought-provoking lyrics, "And is it really so strange? Oh, is it really so strange? Oh, is it really so, really so strange?" Here, the repetition of the question "is it really so strange?" prompts introspection about societal norms and expectations. It challenges the notion of what is considered "normal" or acceptable, urging listeners to question societal conventions and the boundaries they impose. This theme can be linked to embracing one's individuality and defying societal pressures to conform.A particularly striking verse is, "I left the South, I traveled North, I got confused, I killed a horse, I can't help the way I feel." This lyric represents a sense of inner turmoil and confusion. It speaks to the volatile nature of emotions, highlighting the struggle to control or understand them. The mention of killing a horse adds a touch of surrealism to the song and can be interpreted as a metaphor for destructive actions motivated by intense emotions.Towards the end of the song, the line "I lost my bag in Newport Pagnell" stands out due to its apparent simplicity. This lyric could symbolize the loss or shedding of burdens and the liberation that comes with it. It signifies a detachment from material possessions and suggests a newfound freedom from the constraints of materialism.Ultimately, the overarching theme of "Is It Really So Strange?" centers around the profound and often inexplicable nature of love and the resilience it evokes in individuals. Each lyric discussed has contributed to these themes, exploring various aspects of love, courage, resilience, societal conventions, emotional turmoil, and personal liberation.The song invites listeners to reflect on their own experiences and emotions, encouraging them to embrace the complexities of love and the strength that can be found in vulnerability. "Is It Really So Strange?" is a poetic masterpiece that lingers in the soul, reminding us of the profound connections we forge with others and the enduring power of love in our lives.
