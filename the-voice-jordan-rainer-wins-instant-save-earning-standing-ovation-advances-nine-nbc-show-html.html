The Voice: Jordan Rainer wins instant save after earning standing ovation and advances to top nine o

Jordan Rainer was instantly saved on Tuesday's episode of The Voice as the field was narrowed down to the top nine on the NBC singing competition show. 

The 33-year-old singer from Atoka, Oklahoma, hugged the other finalists and then her coach Reba McEntire, 68.

Jordan wowed the crowd singing Blame It On Your Heart by Patty Loveless to win her place to remain in the competition. The audience gave her a standing ovation when she finished.

'That was an amazing performance,' Niall Horan, 30, said. 'I think it would be a real, real shame to lose a personality like Jordan in this competition.'

'I totally agree,' Reba said. 'This is a shame if she goes home tonight. You have worked your butt off. You are so talented. You play so many instruments. Your personality is perfect for America.'

When asked what Reba had taught her as her coach, Jordan didn't hesitate to answer.

'So far the most important thing she has taught me, Reba, that you have taught me, is to stay curious, to stay driven, to stay passionate and to stay as true to who I am and I plan on doing every one of those things,' Jordan said.

Gwen Stefani , 54, opened the show performing her new single True Babe. Gwen, dressed in a embellished red jacket and matching pants, delivered her emotional song surrounded by a field of flowers.

Gwen wasn't the only coach who performed. Reba also took that stage and sang her hit Seven Minutes In Heaven. The crowd cheered for Reba when she finished her touching song in which she also shared a photo of her mother.

Show host Carson Daly, 50, asked Mara Justine, 21, from Galloway New Jersey, how Niall's coaching had helped her before the results were announced.

'I feel like him really believing in me has really helped with my confidence on stage,' Mara said.

The first three contestants that were safe were Team Legend's soul singer from Arkansas Mac Royals, 31, from Wrightsville; Team Gwen's country soul artist Bias, 23, from Chattanooga, Tennessee who sang Johnny Cash song God's Gonna Cut You Down; and Team Reba's Ruby Leigh, 16, from Foley, Missouri who had performed Reba's hit You Lie.

'Thank you, thank you,' Mac said as he walked off the stage.

'I did it!' Lila said.

Team Niall's New York dark pop rocker Nini Iris, 27, from Brooklyn, told Carson it would mean so much to her if she moved on in the competition.

Team Niall's Virginia blues rocker Huntley, 33, from Fredericksburg, who sang With A Little Help From My Friends by Joe Cocker for his performance, found out next that he had been saved. Niall stood up and applauded him and then the two of them hugged.
